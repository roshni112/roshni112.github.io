Namaste/Namaskaram/Namaskar/Namaskara

About

It is standard to always greet each other with namaste or namaskaram. The greeting is for everyone - even strangers. Namaste can be just a casual form of greeting but it can also be formal, a cultural convention or an act of worship.

It is performed by placing the two palms in front of the chest and bowing the head whilst saying the word namaste. In Sanskrit, namaste means "I bow to divinity in you, my greetings, salutations or prostration to you".

Diya (candle/lamp)

About

Diya is another word for candle or lamp and is to be lit with fire. Light symbolizes knowledge and is used to chase away evil, darnkess and ignorance. The Lord, who is the "knowledge principal" (chaitanya), is the source and the illuminator of all knowledge. As a result, light is worshipped as the Lord himself. Knowledge removes ignorance just as light removes darkness. 

Tilak/Bindi

About

The majority of religious people - especially maried women - wear a tilak, bindi or bottu on the forehead. Tilak (which can be seen in the picture) is the application of the Chandan, kumkum or bhasma (vibhooti) which is offered to God and is taken back as prasad (blessings).

The Ajna Chankra, which is the spot between the eyebrows, is known as the energy center for the mind. The tilak is placed there to cool the forehead, protect wisdom and prevent energy loss.
